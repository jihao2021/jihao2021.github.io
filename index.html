Introduction

Dr. Hao Ji is currently a Data Scientist at USC’s Center for Advanced Research Computing. He is also a part-time lecturer at Viterbi teaching 'High Performance Computing in Applied Machine Learning'. He obtained his Ph.D from University of Southern California and his Master's from University of California at Berkeley. He serves as a reviewer for multiple top-tier journals and conferences and holds a position on the Editorial Board of the Journal of Mathematics and Computer Science. Hao’s research focuses on multi-agent reinforcement learning, self-organizing systems, deep learning algorithms, large language models, high performance computing, and time series analysis. If you are interested in conducting research with Hao Ji, please send an email to haoji@usc.edu.

Research Interests

My research interests include game theory, machine learning and AI, time series classification, reinforcement learning, large language models, and high performance computing.

  • Game Theory in Multi-Agent Marine Ship Combat Scenarios
  • Digital Twin Manufacturing System Modeling using Reinforcement Learning
  • Multivariable Time Series Classification using LLM/CNN
  • Surrogate Model for Parameter Optimization of RL Models
  • Using RAG (Retrieval Augmented Generation) for Question & Answer ChatBOT in HPC User Support
  • Agent Based Framework for Autonomous Workflow Development
  • LLM Benchmarks for 3D Modeling and Code Generation
  • Distributed GPU Training and HPC System Benchmarking
